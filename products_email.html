Frequently Asked Questions
about Email Validation API

What is the email verification API?

Veriwhale's email verification API is a powerful tool that instantly determines the validity and potential risk
associated with an email address. Whether used in real-time or asynchronously, this capability enables you to
efficiently detect and suppress invalid or disposable email addresses. By incorporating VeriWhale's API into
your system, you can cleanse your email list, minimize bounce rates, and significantly enhance the delivery
rate for genuine users. For even more comprehensive user checks, the integration of VeriWhale's email
verification API with IP geolocation and phone number validation APIs can provide a robust solution for verifying new user information.

What kind checks does the email verification API do?

VeriWhale's email verification API employs an array of advanced and regularly updated techniques to effectively
identify and suppress invalid or risky email addresses. These techniques encompass various aspects such as syntax
error and typo detection (e.g., john@gnamil.com), real-time checks against SMTP and MX records of the email domain,
sophisticated regular expression (regex) validations, and a diverse range of filters supported by machine learning
algorithms. With this comprehensive approach, VeriWhale ensures that your email verification process is thorough,
accurate, and capable of detecting and mitigating any potential issues with invalid or risky emails.

What other options does the email validation API provide for filtering?

In addition, VeriWhale's email verification API offers the capability to identify and flag various attributes of an
email. This includes distinguishing whether the email originates from a free email provider like Yahoo or Gmail, a
disposable email service such as Yopmail, or a "role" email address like team@ or @sales@. Moreover, VeriWhale
assists in detecting emails associated with recently registered domains, even if they pass other filters. The
identification of recently registered domains and emails aids in flagging potentially fraudulent, spammy, or
suspicious users, enabling you to review them thoroughly.
